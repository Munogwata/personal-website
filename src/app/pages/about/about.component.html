You can call me Muno I was born in 1997, the same year Steve Jobs returned to Apple. I have grown up in different countries ranging from Florida, USA; to Nairobi, Kenya, and I am now based in Sunny South Africa.

As long as I can remember, I have always had a dualistic interest in science, technology, engineering and mathematics (“ STEM”) and design, however I never knew how to pair these two interests together. The fact society likes to juxtapose STEM and creativity did not help either. Therefore, deciding what to study at university was tricky, all I knew for sure is that I wanted to make a positive impact in the world. With this in mind, at the naive age of 16, I thought studying law would be a good idea.

Following the completion of my LLB from the University of Pretoria, I underwent two years of legal training in a Big Law Firm in South Africa. I call this my “Kafka period”, after the iconic Franz Kafka, this is because I think we share a similar perspective in regard to the practice of law, and have an equal enthusiasm for writing. Also my legal career felt very Kafkaesque to say the least.

Nonetheless, I am very grateful for the experience I gained during my legal career. During my legal training I worked on a lot of high profile transactions and legal matters. Furthermore, this period re-affirmed my commitment to creating and designing high quality digital products. During my articles I continued to up-skill and advance as a designer and a developer.

In addition, I continued to expand my portfolio of published articles and had the great opportunity to deliver a Tedx Talk. I used these mediums to further explore my love for STEM and creativity, and use them as opportunities to develop innovative solutions for pressing global social and economic problems. My communication skills, which have only been further sharpened by my legal experience, have allowed me to be regarded as an “economic storyteller”, and have granted me to build one of the top personal finance blogs in South Africa (as according to Daily Finance UK).

My continuous commitment to developing global solutions as a social entrepreneur, economic storyteller, a designer and developer have not gone unnoticed, as I have been fortunate to have been selected in the prestigious Mail and Guardian Top 200 South Africans list, in the class of 2021.
