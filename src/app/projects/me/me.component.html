04 Get to know me - Personal Website 01 Should I Invest? Title: Get to know me - Munozovepi.com Role:UI Designer, Product Manager, Web Developer, and Copy Writer. Timeline: 27 December 2021 to 04 February 2022. Objectives Designing my own personal website that serves as a digital curriculum vitae (“CV”) that supplements my Linkedin profile and standard CV, as it is able to go into more detail about the vast experience I have gained in projects that I have developed and completed.

I want the website to be representative of my skills set and what value I add to any team and company, particularly as it relates to product development, which is the role I believe is most compatible with my experience and skill set.

Furthermore, I believe that it is important that personality and creativity really shine through my personal website. Lastly, it is important that the design of the website is seamless and easy to navigate through. In addition, it is important that all viewers of my website are able to instantly develop a solid and accurate representation of my values, work ethic and ambition within a few seconds of browsing my website.
Design Principles
  1. Grab and retain the attention of the viewers within in a few seconds of them accessing the website.
  2. Clearly outline the job roles and experience that I have.
  3. Clearly demonstrate what distinguishes me from other candidates.
  4. Clearly show my commitment to high quality work and attention to details.
Design Requirements
  1. Easy access to the projects that I am showcasing;
  2. Reference to my professional accounts such as LinkedIn and Github; and
  3. An easy way to get in touch with me as needed.
 Design Process & UI Design Files I used a mood board that I created with Milanote to get an idea of the aesthetic and user interface I am trying to achieve with my personal website.

From the Moodbaord I started to build out the cornerstones of my design system, the colour scheme and the typography.

I then proceed to design the website expanding from the wireframe that I sketched out on paper.

I loved the initial design, but I believe that I could benefit from having less features, in light of my design principles for this project, particularly that of grabbing the user’s attention in a few seconds. Too strike a balance between enough information and a simple user interface, I provided users with the opportunity to sign up to my monthly newsletter. The newsletter is included in the final design of the website but has not been included in the current version of the website. This is because I am currently drafting the privacy policy and ensuring all data, such as the emails received through my personal website is managed and secured in accordance to statutory requirements and best practices.
In addition, I want to custom design the newsletter backend and frontend. Therefore all tasks relating to the development of the newsletter are in the project backlog.
