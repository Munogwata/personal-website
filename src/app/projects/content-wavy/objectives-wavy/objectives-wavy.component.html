The objectives of Wavy can be grouped into two main categories:
  1. Develop users confidence through financial education; and
  2. Drive financial inclusion through financial education.
 1. Develop users confidence through financial education. Wavy wants to empower users to make confident and informed financial decisions, particularly as it relates to investing.

Wavy wants to ensure that users are empowered to make informed financial decisions that allow them to build a profitable investment portfolio. This breaks down to the following objectives:
  1. Improve the user’s familiarity with the capital markets;
  2. Build the confidence of the user, such that they are able to invest in the capital markets;
  3. Aid the user in achieving their personal finance objectives, such as building wealth;
  4. Improve the user’s investment portfolio performance;
  5. Help improve and develop the user’s financial literacy; and
  6. Help users build, develop and maintain important financial habits.

2. Drive financial inclusion and subsequently facilitate sustainable social and economic growth.
Wavy wants to ensure that through financial education, financial inclusion is scaled. This breaks down into the following objectives:
  1. Wavy wants to increase consumer investment in the capital markets;
  2. Wavy wants to facilitate financial and economic inclusion in the capital markets, through investments;
  3. Wavy wants to increase the wealth in households and fight the growing inequality gap; and
  4. Wavy wants consumers to have knowledge to take advantage of the financial benefits that come with investing in the capital markets, such as tax breaks.

It can also be noted that both categories of the objectives of Wavy are all done with the intent of strengthening the capital markets, which plays a critical role in facilitating and ensuring sustainable economic growth. Therefore, it can be concluded that all of Wavy’s objectives can be umbrellaed under the fundamental objective of aiding equitable economic development.
